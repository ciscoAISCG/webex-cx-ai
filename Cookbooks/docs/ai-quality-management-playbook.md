### 1.1 The Market Problem

#### The Three Gaps the Quality Intelligence Suite Closes

**Gap 1: The Visibility Gap**

Traditional QM reviews less than 2–4% of interactions. The 96% that goes unseen contains compliance violations, coaching opportunities, customer frustration signals, and performance patterns that never reach a supervisor's attention. This is not a resourcing failure — it is an architectural one. Manual processes were never designed for the interaction volumes of the modern contact center.

*What the Suite Delivers:* Real-time transcription captures every word of every interaction. AI evaluation scoring assesses every completed interaction against defined criteria. 100% visibility, not 4%.

**Gap 2: The Satisfaction Gap**

Customer satisfaction surveys reach, on average, fewer than 10% of customers — and the customers who respond are disproportionately those with strong feelings, either very positive or very negative. The 90% of customers in the middle — the ones who did not complain but also did not feel delighted — are invisible to the CSAT model. Leaders are making CX decisions on a skewed, incomplete data set.

*What the Suite Delivers:* AutoCSAT uses AI-driven sentiment analysis to predict customer satisfaction for 100% of interactions. Not the 10% who responded to your survey. Every single customer. Every single conversation.

**Gap 3: The Coaching Gap**

Even when supervisors identify a quality issue, the path from issue identification to meaningful coaching intervention is slow, labour-intensive, and often generic. Supervisors spend the majority of their QM time on audit activities — reviewing calls, maintaining spreadsheets, resolving score disputes — rather than on the coaching conversations that actually develop agents. When coaching does happen, it is driven by summary scores rather than specific, individual, evidence-backed insights.

*What the Suite Delivers:* Coaching Insights synthesizes evaluation scores, sentiment data, speech analytics, AutoCSAT predictions, and performance trends into individual, AI-generated coaching recommendations for every agent — with evidence linked directly to the specific interactions that support each insight.

#### The Compounding Power of the Suite

Here is the insight that separates a suite conversation from a product conversation:

**Each capability makes every other capability more powerful.**

- Real-time transcription makes sentiment analysis possible
- Sentiment analysis enriches AI evaluation scoring with emotional context
- AI evaluation scoring feeds coaching insights with quality evidence
- Speech analytics adds behavioural context (talk ratio, silence, cross-talk) to coaching insights
- AutoCSAT connects quality evaluation to predicted customer outcome
- Coaching insights synthesize all five layers into a single, actionable agent development view

When a supervisor sits down with an agent for a coaching conversation, they are not looking at a single metric. They are looking at:

- The agent's AI evaluation score trend over the past month
- The specific questions where the agent consistently scores low
- The sentiment scores on the interactions where quality was lowest
- The speech analytics showing talk ratio and silence patterns
- The AutoCSAT prediction on the interactions where the customer experience was weakest
- An AI-generated coaching tip that connects all of this into a single, prioritized recommendation

**That is not a quality management tool. That is a coaching intelligence engine.**

### 1.3 Capability-by-Capability Value Propositions

#### Capability 1: Real-Time Transcription — The Foundation

**What It Does:** Converts every spoken word in every customer interaction into structured text data in real time. This is the data layer that makes everything else in the suite possible. Without accurate, real-time transcription, AI evaluation cannot assess the interaction, sentiment analysis cannot classify customer emotion, speech analytics cannot measure conversational dynamics, and AutoCSAT cannot predict satisfaction.

**Why It Matters Beyond the Obvious:** Most customers think of transcription as a convenience feature — a record of what was said. In the context of the Quality Intelligence Suite, it is the foundational infrastructure layer. The accuracy and completeness of the transcription directly determines the quality of every downstream insight.

**Key Value Points:**

- Available in 50+ languages — consistent quality intelligence across global contact center operations
- Real-time availability — transcription enables real-time assist, mid-call coaching triggers, and immediate post-call analysis
- The prerequisite for sentiment analysis — sentiment is derived from transcript text, not audio
- Enables AI evaluation scoring on every interaction — no transcript means no evaluation
- Supports agent self-review — agents can read their own interaction transcripts for self-coaching

**Bridge to Next Capability:**

*"Real-time transcription gives us the raw data of every conversation. The next question is: what was the customer feeling during that conversation? That is where sentiment analysis comes in."*

#### Capability 2: Sentiment Analysis — The Emotional Intelligence Layer

**What It Does:** Analyses customer utterances throughout the interaction and classifies the overall conversation sentiment as Positive, Neutral, or Negative — with a confidence score between -100 and +100. Delivered in near real time.

**The Technical Pipeline:**

| **Stage** | **What Happens** | **Example** |
|----|----|----|
| Input | Customer transcript utterances from real-time transcription | *"That's so helpful, thank you!"* |
| Tokenization | Text broken into processable tokens | *["That's", "so", "helpful"...] → [42, 197, 88...]* |
| Contextual Analysis | AI evaluates meaning and context — not just keywords | Full conversational arc analyzed |
| Score Calculation | Sentiment predicted with confidence score | *Positive: 92% / Neutral: 6% / Negative: 2%* |

**Sentiment Thresholds:**

- Above +45 → Positive
- Between -45 and +45 → Neutral
- Below -45 → Negative

**Why It Matters Beyond Basic Sentiment:** Sentiment data is most powerful when it is connected to the other suite capabilities:

- Connected to **AI evaluation scores**: Which quality failures correlate most strongly with negative sentiment?
- Connected to **AutoCSAT**: How does sentiment during the interaction predict post-call satisfaction?
- Connected to **coaching insights**: Which agents consistently generate negative sentiment and what evaluation question patterns explain it?
- Connected to **speech analytics**: Does silence or cross-talk correlate with sentiment decline?

**The Insight That Moves Customers:**

*"You can have an agent who scores 85% on their evaluation form but consistently generates negative sentiment in their interactions. Your current QM tool would show you a strong performer. The suite shows you the full picture — and tells you exactly where the disconnect is."*

**Bridge to Next Capability:**

*"Sentiment tells us how the customer felt. AI evaluation scoring tells us why — what specific agent behaviors drove that emotional outcome. Those two data points together are the foundation of a coaching conversation that actually changes behavior."*

#### Capability 3: AI Evaluation Scoring — The Quality Intelligence Core

**What It Does:** Automatically evaluates every completed interaction against supervisor-defined evaluation criteria. AI scores each question on the evaluation form, provides a written justification with transcript references, and generates an overall evaluation percentage. Supervisors can review, accept, or override any AI score. Every override retrains the model.

**The Trainable Model Advantage — Explained Simply:**

*"Most AI tools score interactions against a generic definition of quality — what a large language model thinks good customer service looks like. Webex AI QM scores interactions against your definition of quality. The evaluation questions are yours. The scoring criteria are yours. And every time a supervisor overrides an AI score, the model learns from that correction and gets a little closer to your standards. Over time, you are not running our AI. You are running your AI — calibrated to your business, your customers, your way of doing things."*

**The Mixture of Models Architecture — Why It Matters:** Not all evaluation questions are created equal. Some questions require classification (did the agent greet the customer — yes or no?). Others require contextual understanding (was the agent empathetic in this specific situation?). Others require judgment (was the transfer actually necessary given the context?).

Webex AI QM dynamically selects the best-performing model for each question type:

- Fine-tuned DistilBERT for classification questions — fast, accurate, cost-efficient
- Tuned statistical classifiers for specific pattern recognition questions
- Azure OpenAI GPT models for contextual and judgment questions

This means every question in every evaluation form is being assessed by the model architecture best suited to answer it — not a single model applied uniformly to all question types.

**For Human AND AI Agents:** The same evaluation framework, the same quality standards, the same coaching intelligence — applied across the entire contact center workforce. Human agents and AI agents evaluated on equal footing, with a unified quality view for supervisors.

**Bridge to Next Capability:**

*"AI evaluation scoring tells us what happened in the interaction against defined quality criteria. Speech analytics tells us how it happened — the conversational dynamics, the silences, the moments where the conversation became unbalanced. Together, they give supervisors a complete behavioral picture."*

#### Capability 4: Speech Analytics — The Behavioural Intelligence Layer

**What It Does:** Analyses the conversational dynamics of every interaction — talk ratio, cross-talk, silence patterns — to surface behavioural signals that evaluation scores alone cannot capture.

**Core Metrics:**

| **Metric** | **What It Measures** | **What It Reveals** |
|----|----|----|
| **Talk Ratio** | % of conversation time agent vs. customer is speaking | Listening skills; whether agent is dominating or under-contributing to the conversation |
| **Cross-Talk** | Moments where agent and customer are speaking simultaneously | Interruption patterns; friction in the conversation; agent impatience signals |
| **Silence** | Moments of extended silence during the interaction | Agent uncertainty; hold time without notice; processing gaps; customer frustration pauses |

**The "Metrics to Watch" Feature:** Automatically surfaces agent outliers against team averages — agents whose speech analytics metrics deviate significantly from the team norm. Supervisors do not have to hunt for anomalies. The system identifies them automatically.

**Why Speech Analytics Matters in the Suite Context:** Evaluation forms tell you what the agent said. Speech analytics tells you how they said it. An agent can technically comply with every evaluation criterion while still creating a poor customer experience through constant interruptions, excessive silence, or a lopsided talk ratio that makes the customer feel unheard.

Combined with sentiment analysis and AI evaluation scoring, speech analytics adds the behavioural dimension that completes the picture of interaction quality.

**Bridge to Next Capability:**

*"We now know what happened, how the customer felt, how the agent performed against quality criteria, and how the conversation flowed behaviourally. The next question is: what did all of that mean for customer satisfaction? That is what AutoCSAT tells us."*

#### Capability 5: AutoCSAT — The Customer Outcome Intelligence Layer

**What It Does:** Uses AI-driven sentiment analysis and interaction data to predict customer satisfaction scores for 100% of interactions — including the 90% of customers who never complete a post-call survey.

**How It Works:** AutoCSAT is trained on post-call survey data from customers who do respond to surveys. It learns the correlation between interaction characteristics (sentiment patterns, evaluation scores, speech analytics metrics, interaction duration, transfer frequency) and actual CSAT outcomes. It then applies that learned correlation to predict CSAT for every interaction — regardless of whether the customer submitted a survey.

**Configuration:**

- Post-call surveys using Experience Management or Global Variables are required to train the initial model
- AutoCSAT configuration available from Control Hub
- Prediction scores available in supervisor desktop and via Search API

**Why AutoCSAT Changes the CX Measurement Conversation:**

Traditional CSAT measurement has three fundamental problems:

1.  **Coverage**: 5–15% survey response rates mean 85–90% of customer satisfaction data is missing
2.  **Bias**: Survey respondents skew toward emotionally extreme experiences — very positive or very negative — the middle 70% of customers are largely invisible
3.  **Lag**: Survey data arrives hours or days after the interaction — too late for real-time intervention

AutoCSAT solves all three simultaneously:

- **Coverage**: 100% of interactions receive a predicted CSAT score
- **Bias**: Prediction is based on the actual interaction data, not self-selection by the customer
- **Lag**: Prediction is available immediately after the interaction completes — enabling rapid intervention

**The Strategic Value:**

*"When you have predicted CSAT for 100% of interactions, you can answer questions that post-call surveys can never answer. Which agents are consistently generating low-satisfaction interactions — not just the ones whose customers happened to respond to your survey? Which interaction types correlate most strongly with satisfaction decline? Which quality evaluation criteria have the strongest impact on predicted customer outcome? The suite connects all of these dots automatically."*

**Bridge to Next Capability:**

*"We have the complete picture of every interaction: quality evaluation, customer sentiment, behavioral dynamics, and predicted satisfaction outcome. The final step is turning all of that intelligence into the coaching insight that changes the next conversation. That is what the Coaching Insights capability delivers."*

### The AIQM Journey — Stage by Stage

#### Stage 1: Discovery

**Objective:** Qualify the opportunity, understand the current state, and map customer pain to the right archetype.

**Duration:** One conversation, 45– 60 minutes

**Who Should Be in the Room:**

- Contact Centre operations leader
- QM manager or supervisor lead
- IT representative (if available)

**Opening the Conversation:**

Goal: *how quality management works in your contact center today — what's working well, what's creating friction, and where you feel like you're operating with incomplete visibility.*

**Discovery Question Bank — Full Set:**

*Current State Questions:*

- How do you currently manage quality in your contact center?
- What percentage of your interactions are reviewed each week?
- How many supervisors are dedicated to QM activities?
- What tool are you currently using for quality management?
- How long have you been using that tool?
- Are your QM and WFM tools integrated, or is data reconciled manually?
- What does a typical week look like for one of your supervisors in terms of how they spend their time?

*Pain Point Questions:*

- What are the biggest frustrations your supervisors have with your current QM process?
- When an agent challenges an evaluation score, how does that conversation typically go and how long does it take?
- How do you handle situational tasks, like transfers or payment verification — that are not required on every call i.e. Not Applicable?
- How confident are you that your current evaluation scores reflect actual performance, or do you think they might be skewed by the sample?
- What does your coaching model look like, is it individual and data-driven, or more generic and team-wide?

*AI Readiness Questions:*

- Are you currently deploying AI agents in your contact centre, or is that on your roadmap?
- If AI agents are handling interactions, how are you evaluating the quality of those interactions today?
- How important is it to you that an AI model learns your specific definition of quality, rather than applying a generic standard?

*Business Goal Questions:*

- What does success look like for your contact centre over the next 12 months?
- How important is agent coaching and development to your CX strategy?
- Are you evaluating any consolidation of your technology stack, QM, WFM, analytics?
- If you could change one thing about your current quality management process, what would it be?

*ROI Driver Questions:*

- What is the cost of your current QM operations, in terms of licensing, supervisor hours, and Day-2 integration maintenance?
- What is the impact of poor quality interactions on your business, in terms of CSAT, churn, or compliance risk?
- What is your current agent attrition rate, and how much of that do you attribute to feedback quality and perceived fairness of evaluation?

The Seven Objections — Know Your Responses

1.  We have Calabrio / Verint → *Complement, not replace. Trial alongside it.*
2.  Scores are lower → *That is accuracy, not failure. Real baseline = real coaching.*
3.  Not ready for AI → *Trial one queue. Supervisors stay in control. Model learns from them.*
4.  How accurate is it? → *Calibration Variance metric. Shrinks over time. We prove it.*
5.  Data privacy? → *Models never shared. Never leave Webex cloud. Transparency Note available.*
6.  Interface too complex? → *Supervisor Walkthrough session week one. UI roadmap active.*
7.  Call count discrepancies? → *Data parity check week one. Standard practice. Two hours.*

### 2.1 ROI Calculation Framework

#### The ROI Formula

**ROI (%) = [(Total Annual Benefits − Total Solution Investment) / Total Solution Investment] × 100**

#### Step 1 — Gather Customer Input Data

These are the data points field teams need to collect in discovery. Most customers can provide these numbers quickly:

| **Input Variable** | **How to Ask** | **Where to Use** |
|----|----|----|
| Number of supervisors doing QM | *"How many supervisors are involved in quality management activities?"* | Supervisor time savings calculation |
| Average hours/week per supervisor on manual QM | *"Roughly how many hours per week does a typical supervisor spend on manual call reviews, scoring, and related activities?"* | Supervisor time savings calculation |
| Average fully-loaded supervisor cost ($/year) | Use industry benchmark if customer cannot provide: $65,000–$95,000 | Supervisor time savings calculation |
| Total number of agents in scope | *"How many agents would be in scope for AIQM?"* | Coverage and coaching calculations |
| Current QM tool annual licensing cost | *"What are you currently paying annually for your QM tool?"* | Tool consolidation savings |
| Average score disputes per month | *"How often do agents challenge their evaluation scores?"* | Score dispute time savings |
| Average time to resolve a score dispute (hours) | *"How long does a typical score dispute conversation take to resolve?"* | Score dispute time savings |
| Agent attrition rate (%) | *"What is your current annual agent attrition rate?"* | Attrition cost savings |
| Average cost per agent attrition event ($) | Use industry benchmark if unavailable: $10,000–$20,000 per agent | Attrition cost savings |
| Monthly interaction volume | *"Roughly how many interactions does your contact center handle per month?"* | Coverage value calculation |
| Current CSAT score | *"What is your current CSAT score?"* | CX improvement ROI |
| WFM tool annual cost (if applicable) | *"What are you paying for your workforce management tool?"* | WEM consolidation savings |

---

#### Step 2 — Calculate the Benefits

##### Benefit Category 1: Supervisor Audit Time Savings

Annual Supervisor Audit Hours Saved =

(Supervisors × Hours/Week on Manual QM × 52 weeks) × 45% reduction factor

Annual Dollar Value =

Annual Hours Saved × (Fully-Loaded Annual Supervisor Cost / 2,080 working hours)

*Example: 10 supervisors × 15 hours/week × 52 weeks × 45% = 3,510 hours saved* *3,510 hours × $40/hour fully-loaded = $140,400 annual savings*

---

##### Benefit Category 2: Score Dispute Time Elimination

Annual Score Dispute Hours Saved =

Monthly Disputes × 12 months × Average Resolution Time (hours) × 70% reduction factor

Annual Dollar Value =

Annual Hours Saved × Supervisor Hourly Rate

*Example: 50 disputes/month × 12 × 1.5 hours × 70% = 630 hours saved* *630 hours × $40/hour = $25,200 annual savings*

---

##### Benefit Category 3: QM Tool Consolidation

Annual Savings = Current QM Tool Annual Licensing Cost (full elimination where applicable)

*Note: Where WEM consolidation also replaces WFM tool, add WFM tool annual cost*

---

##### Benefit Category 4: Agent Attrition Reduction

Annual Attrition Cost Reduction =

(Total Agents × Current Attrition Rate × Cost Per Attrition Event) × 15% reduction factor

*Example: 500 agents × 25% attrition × $15,000 per event × 15% = $281,250 annual savings*

*Note: Attrition reduction is driven by improved coaching quality, agent self-visibility into performance, and reduced score dispute friction — all of which improve agent engagement and perceived fairness*

---

##### Benefit Category 5: CSAT Improvement Revenue Impact

Annual Revenue Impact =

Revenue at Risk from Poor CX × CSAT Improvement % × Revenue Retention Rate

Or alternatively:

Customer Lifetime Value × Estimated Churn Reduction %

*Note: Use customer's own CSAT-to-revenue correlation data where available. Use industry benchmarks (10–20% CSAT improvement typical) where not.*

---

##### Benefit Category 6: Compliance Risk Avoidance

Annual Compliance Risk Value =

Estimated Cost of Compliance Incident × Annual Incident Probability Reduction %

*Note: This is highly variable by industry. For financial services and healthcare, this is often the largest single ROI driver. Use industry benchmark data for regulatory fine averages where available.*
